Repotting Houseplants: A Quick Guide

Most houseplants need a new pot every one to two years, though fast growers like pothos or spider plants may need it sooner. The clearest sign is roots circling the bottom of the pot or poking through the drainage holes.

Signs it's time

  • Water runs straight through without soaking in
  • Growth has slowed noticeably during the growing season
  • The plant dries out much faster than it used to
  • Roots are visible at the surface or through drainage holes

Choosing a pot

Go up one size at a time — roughly two inches larger in diameter. A pot that's too big holds excess moisture the roots can't use, which invites rot. Always make sure the new pot has drainage.

Steps

  1. Water the plant a day before repotting so the root ball slides out easily
  2. Gently loosen compacted roots around the edges
  3. Add fresh potting mix to the bottom of the new pot
  4. Center the plant and fill in around the sides
  5. Water thoroughly and keep out of direct sun for a few days
